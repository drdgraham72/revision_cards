Motion & Forces Q1–8 · 15 marks
Vectors
Magnitude + direction: velocity, force, acceleration, displacement, momentum
Scalars
Magnitude only: speed, distance, mass, energy, time, power, temperature
SUVAT (HT)
v=u+at; s=ut+½at²; v²=u²+2as; s=(u+v)t/2
Newton 1
Object stays at rest or constant velocity unless resultant force acts
Newton 2
F=ma; resultant force = mass × acceleration
Newton 3
Action-reaction pairs equal and opposite
Terminal velocity
When drag force = weight; no resultant force → constant speed
Distance-time graph: gradient = speed. Velocity-time graph: gradient = acceleration, area = distance
Conservation of Energy Q9–12 · 12 marks
Conservation
Energy cannot be created/destroyed, only transferred/converted
Kinetic
EK = ½mv²; increases with speed²
Gravitational PE
EP = mgh; depends on height and mass
Elastic PE
EP = ½kx²; stored in stretched/compressed springs
Efficiency
(Useful energy output / Total energy input) × 100%
Power
P = E/t = W/t; rate of energy transfer (Watts)
Work done
W = F × d (force parallel to displacement)
Efficiency ≤ 100%. "Lost" energy becomes heat (dissipated), not destroyed
Waves Q13–16 · 12 marks
Transverse
Oscillations ⊥ to direction: light, water, EM waves
Longitudinal
Oscillations ∥ to direction: sound, P-waves
Wave equation
v = f × λ; speed = frequency × wavelength
Reflection
Angle of incidence = angle of reflection
Refraction
Change in speed → change in direction at boundary
Diffraction
Waves spread out after passing through gap/obstacle
Seismic P-waves
Longitudinal, fast, travel through all materials
Seismic S-waves
Transverse, slower, solids only
Sound needs medium; EM waves travel through vacuum
Electromagnetic Spectrum Q17–20 · 11 marks
Order (low→high)
Radio → Microwave → IR → Visible → UV → X-ray → Gamma
Increasing
Wavelength ↓, Frequency ↑, Energy ↑
Radio waves
Long wavelength, low energy, communication/TV
Microwaves
Satellite comms, mobile phones, cooking
Infrared
Thermal imaging, remote controls, heating
Visible light
ROYGBIV; reflection/refraction for vision
Ultraviolet
Sunbeds, security marking, vitamin D
X-rays
Medical imaging, airport security
Gamma rays
Cancer treatment, sterilisation
UV/X-rays/gamma = ionising radiation → DNA damage, cancer risk
