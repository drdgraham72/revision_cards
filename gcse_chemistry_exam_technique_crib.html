Exam Technique — Higher Mark Questions

Edexcel GCSE Chemistry 1CH0/1H · 2–6 mark extended answers
6-mark Extended Writing: Structure & Bonding → Separation Q9c · *asterisk = QWC
trigger words: "explain… in terms of structure and bonding… how this difference is used to…"
"Explain the difference in boiling points of NaCl and water and how this is used to separate them."
1
Structure NaCl — giant ionic lattice; Na⁺ and Cl⁻ ions held by strong electrostatic forces in all directions
2
Consequence — needs huge energy to overcome these forces → very high boiling point (1465°C)
3
Structure H₂O — simple molecular; held together by weak intermolecular forces (hydrogen bonds)
4
Consequence — little energy needed → low boiling point (100°C)
5
Method: distillation — heat to 100°C; water boils and evaporates; NaCl stays in flask
6
Collect — cool and condense steam → pure water; pure dry NaCl remains in flask
Don't say "stronger bonds" — say "stronger intermolecular forces" for molecular substances. Ionic = electrostatic attractions between ions, NOT bonds.
Asterisk (*) = Quality of Written Communication marked. Write in full sentences. Logic must flow: structure → bonding → property → application.
6-mark Compare & Contrast: Pure Metals vs Alloys Q10b · *asterisk
trigger words: "compare and contrast… include an explanation of similarities and differences…"
"Compare pure Al and Cu with their alloys. Explain similarities and differences in properties and uses."
1
Pure metals — regular layers of same-size atoms; layers slide over each other easily → soft and malleable
2
Alloys — different-sized atoms disrupt regular layers; sliding prevented → harder and stronger
3
Al similarity — both conduct electricity and heat (delocalised electrons in both)
4
Al difference — pure Al soft; duralumin (alloy) used for aircraft/bikes where strength needed
5
Cu similarity — both conduct; metallic bonding retained in alloy
6
Cu difference — pure Cu best conductor → wiring; brass (alloy) harder → instruments, fittings
Must address BOTH metals and BOTH pure + alloy to access all 6 marks. Examiners mark against a "ladder" — each level needs previous level covered.
Never say alloys are "a mixture of metals" without explaining WHY that makes them harder. Mechanism = different atom sizes → disrupts layers. That's the mark.
4-mark Mole Calculation: Gas Volume Q10a(ii)
trigger words: "calculate the maximum volume… of [gas] produced from [mass] of [compound]"
"Calculate the max volume in cm³ of N₂ from 110g of NaN₃. (Mr NaN₃ = 65; 1 mol gas = 24 dm³)"
1
mol NaN₃ = 110 ÷ 65 = 1.692 mol
2
Ratio from equation 2NaN₃ → 3N₂: mol N₂ = 1.692 × (3/2) = 2.538 mol
3
vol = 2.538 × 24 = 60.92 dm³
4
convert: × 1000 = 60 923 cm³
The dm³ → cm³ conversion is a dedicated mark. Forgetting it = 1 mark dropped instantly. Also: check the molar ratio carefully — it's 3/2 here, not 1:1.
Even if final answer wrong, method marks awarded for correct steps shown. Write every step. Never skip molar ratio line — that's where 1 mark lives.
4-mark Atom Economy Calculation Q4a
trigger words: "calculate the atom economy for the production of [product] in this reaction"
"Cu₂S + O₂ → 2Cu + SO₂. Calculate atom economy for SO₂. Give to 2 sig figs."
1
Identify desired product: SO₂, Mr = 64
2
Sum all products: 2Cu = 2×63.5 = 127; SO₂ = 64; total = 191
3
AE = 64/191 × 100 = 33.5% → 34% (2 s.f.)
Common error: using sum of REACTANTS as denominator instead of sum of PRODUCTS. The formula is Mr(desired) ÷ ΣMr(all products) × 100.
Always apply sig figs at the end as instructed — "2 sig figs" is a separate mark. 33.5 → 34%.
also watch forPercentage yield uses actual/theoretical — completely different formula. Don't mix them up under pressure.
3-mark Explain Why pH Changes Q2d(iii)
trigger words: "explain why the pH starts at [low] and ends at [higher value]"
"Explain why pH starts low and ends high when Ca(OH)₂ is added to HCl."
1
Start: HCl is a strong acid — fully dissociates → high concentration of H⁺ ions → low pH
2
During: Ca(OH)₂ provides OH⁻ ions which neutralise H⁺ → H⁺ + OH⁻ → H₂O → pH rises
3
End: excess Ca(OH)₂ → excess OH⁻ ions → alkaline → pH above 7 (up to ~12)
Must name the ions responsible at each stage. "The acid is neutralised" alone = 0 marks. You need H⁺ and OH⁻ explicitly.
3-mark Equilibrium Experiment Design Q5b · devised experiment
trigger words: "devise an experiment to show how position of equilibrium is affected by temperature"
"2NO₂ (brown) ⇌ N₂O₄ (colourless). Use the sealed tube, beaker, kettle, ice."
1
Cold test: place sealed tube in beaker of ice water → observe colour becomes paler/nearly colourless
2
Hot test: place same tube in beaker of hot water (from kettle) → observe colour becomes darker brown
3
Conclusion: cold → equilibrium shifts right (→ N₂O₄) as forward reaction is exothermic; hot → shifts left (← NO₂)
Key structure: apparatus used → observable result → explanation in terms of equilibrium shift. All three parts needed for 3 marks.
Must state the colour change observed, not just "the colour changes." Paler = more N₂O₄. Darker = more NO₂. That's the measurable evidence.
This question type always requires: control (room temp baseline) + hot + cold. Compare all three observations.
3-mark Electrolysis Trend: State & Explain Q6b(i)
trigger words: "state and explain the trend shown in these results"
"Current increases 0→1A; mass of Cu deposited increases 0→0.196g. Explain."
1
State: as current increases, mass of copper deposited increases (proportionally)
2
Explain mechanism: higher current = greater charge per second flowing through circuit
3
Consequence: more Cu²⁺ ions are discharged at cathode per second → more Cu atoms deposited
"State AND explain" = 2 separate things. Stating the trend without the mechanism = max 1 mark. Note data isn't perfectly linear at higher currents — you can mention this as an observation.
3-mark Copper Oxide: When to Stop Adding Q8b
trigger words: "explain how the student knows when to stop adding [excess solid]"
"Adding CuO to H₂SO₄ until max CuSO₄ formed. How does the student know to stop?"
1
The student adds CuO until it no longer disappears — black powder remains visible at the bottom
2
This means all the acid has been used up (CuO now in excess)
3
Filter off the excess CuO to leave a pure copper sulfate solution
The visual cue ("black powder no longer dissolves") + reason ("acid exhausted") + action ("filter") = 3 marks. Each step is its own mark point.
3-mark Particle Model: Solution → Crystal Q8d
trigger words: "describe how the arrangement and movement of particles change as crystals form from solution"
1
Movement: in solution, particles move randomly with high kinetic energy; in crystal, particles vibrate about fixed positions only
2
Arrangement: solution = random/disordered; crystal = regular, ordered lattice (fixed pattern)
3
Distance: particles move closer together as they pack into lattice; intermolecular forces become stronger
Must use the word "arrangement" AND "movement" — the question asks for both. Missing either = max 2 marks regardless of quality.
Universal Technique: The Mark-Hunting Framework
for every multi-mark question
→
Count the marks in brackets. That's how many distinct points you need.
→
For "explain": always give cause → mechanism → consequence (3 = 1 mark each)
→
For "compare": must address BOTH items explicitly. "A is harder than B" beats "A is hard"
→
For calculations: write formula → substitute values → calculate → state units
red-flag command words
State = one fact, no explanation needed. Adding explanation wastes time.
Describe = what happens (not why). Include direction of change, observable evidence.
Explain = what happens + why (mechanism). Name ions/particles responsible.
Predict = apply a pattern from data given to a new case. Show your reasoning.
2-mark Purity & Melting Point Reasoning Q9a(ii)
trigger words: "state whether each sample is pure or impure and justify your answers"
A
160–164°C = range of 4° → IMPURE. Pure substances melt at one sharp temperature; a range indicates impurities present.
B
166°C = single value → PURE. Sharp, single melting point.
C
163–165°C = range → IMPURE. Note: range is narrower than A but still not a single point — still impure.
Justify means link your answer to the data given. "Impure because it has a range" = the full mark. Don't over-explain. Each sample = separate mark.
