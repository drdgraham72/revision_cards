Balancing Equations

Same atoms in, same atoms out. That's it.
Change the BIG numbers (coefficients) in front
NEVER change the small numbers (subscripts) inside a formula
Do this
2H₂O
Put a big 2 in front — means 2 molecules of H₂O
Never this
H₃O
Changing the subscript creates a different substance entirely!

The 4-Step Method

1
Write the unbalanced equation
Write all the correct formulae. Don't change these once written.
2
Count atoms on each side
Make a tally: how many of each element on the left vs right?
3
Add coefficients to balance
Put big numbers in front of formulae until each element matches. Start with the element that appears in the fewest places. Leave H and O until last.
4
Final check
Re-count every atom. Left must equal right. Every single one.

Worked Examples

Balance: Mg + O₂ → MgO
Unbalanced
Mg + O₂ → MgO Mg: 1=1   O: 2≠1
Fix O
O has 2 on left, 1 on right. Put a 2 in front of MgO: Mg + O₂ → 2MgO Mg: 1≠2   O: 2=2
Fix Mg
Now Mg is 1 vs 2. Put a 2 in front of Mg: 2Mg + O₂ → 2MgO Mg: 2=2   O: 2=2   ✔ Balanced!
Balance: Na + H₂O → NaOH + H₂
Unbalanced
Na + H₂O → NaOH + H₂ Na: 1=1   H: 2≠3   O: 1=1
Fix H
H is tricky — it appears in three places. Try 2 in front of H₂O and 2 in front of NaOH: Na + 2H₂O → 2NaOH + H₂ Na: 1≠2   H: 4=4   O: 2=2
Fix Na
Put 2 in front of Na: 2Na + 2H₂O → 2NaOH + H₂ Na: 2=2   H: 4=4   O: 2=2   ✔ Balanced!
Balance: Fe₂O₃ + C → Fe + CO₂
Unbalanced
Fe₂O₃ + C → Fe + CO₂ Fe: 2≠1   O: 3≠2   C: 1=1
Fix Fe
2 Fe on left, 1 on right. Put 2 in front of Fe: Fe₂O₃ + C → 2Fe + CO₂ Fe: 2=2   O: 3≠2   C: 1=1
Fix O
3 O on left, 2 on right. Need 3 on right, so put 3 in front of CO₂ (gives 6 O). Then need 2 Fe₂O₃ for 6 O on left: 2Fe₂O₃ + 3C → 4Fe + 3CO₂ Fe: 4=4   O: 6=6   C: 3=3   ✔ Balanced!

Don't Forget State Symbols

(s)
Solid
MgO(s), NaCl(s)
(l)
Liquid
H₂O(l), Br₂(l)
(g)
Gas
CO₂(g), H₂(g)
(aq)
Aqueous
NaCl(aq), HCl(aq)

Mistakes That Lose Marks Every Time

×
Changing subscripts instead of coefficients. To get 2 oxygen atoms, write H₂O₂ — NO! That's hydrogen peroxide. Write 2H₂O.
×
Forgetting that the coefficient multiplies EVERYTHING. 2H₂SO₄ means 2×H₂SO₄ = 4 H, 2 S, 8 O. Not just 2 H.
×
Not rechecking after balancing one element. Fixing oxygen often unbalances hydrogen. Always recount ALL elements at the end.
×
Forgetting diatomic elements. O₂, H₂, N₂, F₂, Cl₂, Br₂, I₂ — these always come in pairs when they're elements on their own.
×
Missing state symbols. If the question says "include state symbols" and you don't, you lose a mark. Free mark thrown away.
