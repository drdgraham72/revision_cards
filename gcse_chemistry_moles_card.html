See it in action

Example 1: How many moles in 36 g of water (H&sub2;O)?
1
Find Mr of H&sub2;O Mr = (2 × 1) + 16 = 18
2
Use the formula: moles = mass ÷ Mr moles = 36 ÷ 18 = 2 mol
2 moles of water
Example 2: What is the mass of 0.5 mol of NaCl?
1
Find Mr of NaCl Mr = 23 + 35.5 = 58.5
2
Use the formula: mass = moles × Mr mass = 0.5 × 58.5 = 29.25 g
29.25 g of sodium chloride
Example 3: How many atoms in 12 g of carbon (C)?
1
Mr of carbon = 12 Ar = 12
2
Mass → Moles: divide by Mr moles = 12 ÷ 12 = 1 mol
3
Moles → Atoms: multiply by Avogadro's atoms = 1 × 6.02×10²³ = 6.02×10²³
6.02 × 10²³ carbon atoms
Example 4: How many moles in 500 mg of CaCO&sub3;?
1
Convert mg to g FIRST 500 mg ÷ 1000 = 0.5 g
2
Find Mr of CaCO&sub3; Mr = 40 + 12 + (3 × 16) = 100
3
moles = mass ÷ Mr moles = 0.5 ÷ 100 = 0.005 mol
0.005 mol (or 5 × 10&supmin;³ mol)
Example 5: What is the mass of 3.01 × 10²³ atoms of iron (Fe)?
1
Atoms → Moles: divide by Avogadro's moles = 3.01×10²³ ÷ 6.02×10²³ = 0.5 mol
2
Ar of Fe = 56
3
Moles → Mass: multiply by Mr mass = 0.5 × 56 = 28 g
28 g of iron
