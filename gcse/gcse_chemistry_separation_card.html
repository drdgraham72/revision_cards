Which Separation Technique?

Ask yourself these questions. The answer falls out.

Answer the questions — get the technique

Click Yes or No. Three questions maximum.
Filtration
Separates an insoluble solid from a liquid
Pour mixture through filter paper in a funnel. The solid stays on the paper (residue), the liquid passes through (filtrate).
Sand from salty water, calcium carbonate from a reaction mixture
Exam trap: they ask "what is the residue?" (solid) vs "what is the filtrate?" (liquid). Know both words.
Evaporation
Gets a dissolved solid from a solution (when you don't need the liquid)
Heat the solution in an evaporating basin until the water boils off. The solid remains.
Salt from salt water (quick but gives small crystals/powder)
Fast but crude. If you want nice crystals, use crystallisation instead. Evaporation can decompose some solids.
Crystallisation
Gets pure crystals of a dissolved solid from a solution
Heat gently until saturated (crystals form at edges). Stop heating. Leave to cool slowly. Filter off crystals, pat dry.
Copper sulfate crystals, pure salt crystals
Don't boil to dryness! You'll get powder, not crystals. Slow cooling = bigger, purer crystals.
Simple Distillation
Separates a liquid from a solution (when you want the liquid)
Heat solution. Liquid boils, vapour rises into condenser, cools back to liquid, collected in a beaker.
Pure water from salt water, water from ink
Use FRACTIONAL distillation if separating two liquids with different boiling points (e.g. ethanol from water). The fractionating column is the difference.
Chromatography
Separates and identifies substances in a mixture (usually dyes/inks)
Spot sample on pencil baseline. Place paper in solvent (below the line!). Solvent rises, carries substances different distances.
Identifying dyes in food colouring, checking purity of a substance
Baseline in PENCIL (ink would dissolve!). Solvent level BELOW the baseline. One spot = pure, multiple spots = mixture.
Fractional Distillation
Separates two or more miscible liquids with different boiling points
Same as simple distillation but with a fractionating column. Liquid with lowest boiling point comes off first.
Ethanol from water, separating crude oil fractions
Know the difference: SIMPLE = liquid from a dissolved solid. FRACTIONAL = liquid from another liquid.
