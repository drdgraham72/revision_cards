Exam Technique — Higher Mark Questions

Edexcel GCSE Physics Paper 1 · 4–6 mark extended answers
6-mark Extended Writing: Forces & Motion → Resultant Force Q8c · *asterisk = QWC
trigger words: "explain… in terms of forces… how this causes…"
"Explain how the forces acting on a skydiver change during their fall and how this affects their motion."
1
Initial forces — weight (down) and air resistance (up, small initially) → resultant force down → accelerates
2
Increasing speed — air resistance increases with speed (drag force ∝ speed²) → resultant force decreases
3
Terminal velocity — when air resistance = weight → resultant force = 0 → constant velocity (no acceleration)
4
Opens parachute — increases air resistance dramatically → resultant force up → decelerates
5
New terminal velocity — slower speed where air resistance = weight again → safe landing speed
6
Motion change — from acceleration to constant velocity to deceleration → controlled descent
Don't say "gravity increases" — weight is constant. Air resistance changes with speed, not gravity.
Asterisk (*) = Quality of Written Communication marked. Write in full sentences. Logic must flow: forces → resultant → motion → effect.
6-mark Compare & Contrast: Energy Transfers Q12b · *asterisk
trigger words: "compare and contrast… include explanations of…"
"Compare the energy transfers in a hydroelectric power station and a coal-fired power station."
1
Hydroelectric input — gravitational potential energy of water stored in reservoir
2
Hydroelectric transfer — GPE → kinetic energy as water falls → mechanical energy turns turbine → electrical energy in generator
3
Coal-fired input — chemical energy stored in coal
4
Coal-fired transfer — chemical energy → thermal energy (burning) → kinetic energy (steam) → mechanical energy (turbine) → electrical energy
5
Efficiency similarity — both convert primary energy to electrical energy via mechanical intermediate
6
Renewable difference — hydroelectric renewable (water cycle); coal non-renewable (finite fossil fuel) + produces CO₂
Must address BOTH systems and BOTH similarities AND differences to access all 6 marks. Examiners mark against a "ladder" — each level needs previous level covered.
Never say energy is "lost" — it's dissipated (wasted) as heat/sound. Energy conservation: input energy = useful output + wasted energy.
4-mark Calculation with Explanation: SUVAT Equations Q5b · Higher Tier
trigger words: "calculate… and explain how you used the equation…"
"A car accelerates from rest at 2.5 m/s² for 8 seconds. Calculate the distance travelled and explain your working."
1
Identify variables — u = 0 m/s (from rest), a = 2.5 m/s², t = 8 s, s = ?
2
Choose equation — s = ut + ½at² (has s, u, a, t; no v needed)
3
Substitute values — s = (0 × 8) + ½ × 2.5 × (8)² = 0 + 0.5 × 2.5 × 64
4
Calculate — s = 0.5 × 2.5 × 64 = 1.25 × 64 = 80 m
Alternative: v² = u² + 2as
v = u + at = 0 + 2.5 × 8 = 20 m/s
s = (v² - u²)/(2a) = (400 - 0)/(2 × 2.5) = 400/5 = 80 m
Always state the equation before substituting. Show units throughout. For HT: explain why you chose that equation.
Don't mix up s (displacement) and d (distance) — if motion is in straight line, they're the same.
6-mark Extended Writing: Waves → Seismic Waves Q15c · *asterisk
trigger words: "explain… using ideas about waves…"
"Explain how P-waves and S-waves travel through the Earth and how this helps locate earthquakes."
1
P-wave nature — longitudinal waves; particles oscillate parallel to direction of travel (compression/rarefaction)
2
P-wave speed — faster than S-waves; travel through solids, liquids, and gases
3
S-wave nature — transverse waves; particles oscillate perpendicular to direction of travel
4
S-wave limitation — only travel through solids (shear waves need rigidity)
5
Earthquake detection — P-waves arrive first at seismograph, then S-waves (time difference indicates distance)
6
Epicentre location — use time difference from multiple stations → triangulate position
P-waves = Primary (fast, longitudinal); S-waves = Secondary (slower, transverse). Both body waves from focus.
Don't confuse with surface waves (Love/Rayleigh) — these are separate and cause most damage.
6-mark Extended Writing: Electromagnetic Spectrum → Communication Q18b · *asterisk
trigger words: "explain why… is used for… in terms of properties of waves"
"Explain why microwaves are used for satellite communication but gamma rays are not."
1
Microwave properties — long wavelength (cm range), low frequency, low energy photons
2
Microwave advantages — penetrate atmosphere well, reflect off satellite dishes, carry information via modulation
3
Microwave safety — low energy means not harmful to living tissue (unlike higher frequency waves)
4
Gamma ray properties — very short wavelength (picometres), high frequency, very high energy photons
5
Gamma ray problems — absorbed/scattered by atmosphere, too penetrating (dangerous), hard to modulate for data
6
Gamma ray safety — ionising radiation damages cells/DNA → cancer/mutations; unsuitable for communication
Spectrum order: radio → microwave → infrared → visible → ultraviolet → X-ray → gamma. Energy/frequency increases left to right.
Don't say microwaves "don't penetrate atmosphere" — they do penetrate better than higher frequency waves.
4-mark Graph Interpretation: Velocity-Time Q6c
trigger words: "use the graph to calculate… and describe the motion"
"Use the velocity-time graph to calculate the total distance travelled and describe the motion of the object."
1
Identify sections — 0-2s: constant acceleration (gradient = +4 m/s²); 2-4s: constant velocity (0 m/s²); 4-6s: constant deceleration (-2 m/s²)
2
Calculate areas — Triangle 0-2s: ½ × 2 × 8 = 8 m; Rectangle 2-4s: 2 × 8 = 16 m; Triangle 4-6s: ½ × 2 × 8 = 8 m
3
Total distance — 8 + 16 + 8 = 32 m (all areas positive as distance is scalar)
4
Motion description — accelerates from rest, reaches 8 m/s, maintains speed, then decelerates to stop
Distance = total area under v-t graph (always positive). Displacement = algebraic sum of areas (negative areas subtract).
Don't calculate speed from area — area gives distance/displacement, not speed. Speed from gradient.
6-mark Extended Writing: Energy & Efficiency Q11c · *asterisk
trigger words: "explain how… affects the efficiency… in terms of energy transfers"
"Explain how friction affects the efficiency of a car engine and suggest ways to improve efficiency."
1
Energy input — chemical energy in fuel converted to kinetic energy of car
2
Friction losses — mechanical friction in engine, tyres on road, air resistance → dissipated as thermal energy (heat)
3
Efficiency definition — useful output energy / total input energy × 100%
4
Efficiency reduction — friction converts useful KE to wasted heat → less energy available for motion → lower efficiency
5
Improvement methods — reduce friction: oil lubrication, smooth surfaces, aerodynamic design, electric motors (no moving parts)
6
Alternative systems — regenerative braking recaptures KE; hybrid systems more efficient than petrol alone
Efficiency = (useful power output / total power input) × 100%. Never greater than 100% due to conservation of energy.
Don't say "energy is lost" — it's transferred to thermal energy (dissipated). Total energy is conserved.
4-mark Calculation: Wave Equation Q14b
trigger words: "calculate… using the wave equation…"
"A wave has frequency 50 Hz and wavelength 4 m. Calculate the speed of the wave."
1
Recall equation — wave speed = frequency × wavelength (v = f × λ)
2
Identify values — f = 50 Hz, λ = 4 m
3
Substitute — v = 50 × 4 = 200 m/s
4
Units check — Hz × m = (1/s) × m = m/s ✓
Rearranged: f = v/λ or λ = v/f
If speed = 340 m/s, frequency = 256 Hz, then λ = 340/256 ≈ 1.33 m
Always show the equation, substitution, and units. Frequency in Hz, wavelength in m, speed in m/s.
Don't confuse with sound speed (330-340 m/s in air) or light speed (3×10⁸ m/s).
