GCSE English Language — Cross-Board Crib Sheet

Core skills · what examiners reward · ambitious language
Paper 1: Reading & Analysing Texts 24 marks total
Q1 (4 marks)
Retrieve explicit information — isolated facts from the text; answers are in the text, word-for-word or paraphrased
Q2 (8 marks)
Infer meaning — read between the lines; evidence from text + what it suggests (not literally stated)
Q3 (8 marks)
Language analysis — HOW does the writer achieve an effect? Use PEDAL: P=point, E=evidence, D=device/technique, A=analysis, L=link to effect
Q4 (4 marks)
Summarise two sources — compress key ideas from passages A & B into ~50 words each
Inference ≠ guessing. Find clues in: word choice, tone, structure, what's omitted, what's emphasized
Language Devices: Recognition & Analysis Q3 essential
Metaphor
States one thing IS another.
✓ Ambitious"The city's a sleeping giant" — compares the quiet city to a dormant power
✗ Weak"He was angry like a bear" (simile, not metaphor; also cliché)
Simile
Uses 'like' or 'as'.
✓ Ambitious"Rain hammered down like machine-gun fire" — suggests relentless violence
✗ Weak"Like a fish out of water" (so cliché it's invisible)
Personification
Gives human qualities to objects/ideas.
✓ Ambitious"The wind whispered secrets" — creates intimacy & mystery
✗ Weak"The trees danced" (weak because overdone; effect is vague)
Alliteration
Repeated initial consonant sounds.
✓ Ambitious"Bitter, brittle, broken bonds" — creates rhythm, emphasises fragmentation
✗ Weak"Repeatedly reaching" (if no clear purpose; just phonetic filler)
Hyperbole
Extreme exaggeration.
✓ Ambitious"I've told you a billion times" — comic exaggeration conveys exasperation
✗ Weak"It was very, very hot" (not hyperbole; just repetition)
Structural Techniques also Q3
Positioning
Where information appears — opening hooks attention; ending leaves lasting impression
Juxtaposition
Place contrasts side-by-side for effect. Eg: "The luxury penthouse overlooked the sprawling slums"
Fragmentation
Short. Sharp. Sentences. Creates impact & pace. Analysis: "conveys urgency" or "emphasises key ideas"
Lists
Accumulation of items builds effect. Eg: "Poverty, disease, illiteracy, despair" — rhythm overwhelms; shows scale
Repetition
Repeats phrase/word. Eg: "Never again. Never again will this happen." — creates emphasis & determination
Rhetorical Q
Asks without expecting answer. Eg: "Who really benefits?" — makes reader think; prompts agreement
Analysing Language: The PEDAL Framework top mark quality
✓ STRONG ANALYSIS: "The metaphor 'drowning in paperwork' personalises bureaucracy as a life-threatening force, suggesting the protagonist's sense of helplessness and being overwhelmed by the system."
✗ WEAK ANALYSIS: "The word 'drowning' is a metaphor. It's comparing drowning to paperwork. It's effective."
Key: Don't just NAME the technique. Explain WHAT effect it creates + HOW it makes the reader feel/think
Use modal verbs: suggests, implies, conveys, emphasises, reinforces, highlights, creates, evokes
Paper 2: Writing — Accuracy & Expression 24 marks total
Q5 (40 mins, 24 marks)
Persuasive/explanatory/descriptive writing. Marks: 8 for composition+structure, 8 for language+technique, 8 for punctuation+spelling+grammar
Task focus
Must match purpose: persuasive = convince; explanatory = inform clearly; descriptive = create atmosphere
Audience
Adapt tone, register, formality. For teenagers = conversational confidence; for governors = formal authority
SPAG (8 marks)
Spelling (1–2), Punctuation (2–3), Grammar (2–3), Verb forms. ONLY lose marks for errors that obscure meaning or confuse
Ambitious vocabulary + complex sentence structures + zero errors = marks awarded for expression
Ambitious Language Techniques WRITING GOLD
Pacing
✓ Ambitious"The silence stretched. Seconds became minutes. Unbearable. Suffocating."
✗ Weak"It was very quiet for a long time."
Sensory detail
✓ Ambitious"The coffee's bitterness coated their tongues as rain drummed against the windows."
✗ Weak"They drank coffee while it rained outside."
Precise verbs
✓ Ambitious"She scrutinised every detail." / "Rain lashed the windows."
✗ Weak"She looked at things." / "Rain hit the windows."
Varied vocabulary
✓ Ambitious"The politician's rhetoric manipulated, distorted, and ultimately deceived millions."
✗ Weak"The politician said bad things that were not true."
Complex sentences
✓ Ambitious"While storms ravaged the coast, locals huddled indoors, knowing the worst was yet to come."
✗ Weak"There was a storm. People were inside. It was going to get worse."
Persuasive Techniques ≠ Literary Devices DIFFERENT skill
Rhetorical questions
"Shouldn't every child have access to education?" — triggers agreement
Direct address
"You know the truth" or "Imagine if..." — creates intimacy; involves reader
Statistics/facts
"1 in 5 children..." — lends credibility & weight
Emotive language
"Cruel," "desperate," "heartbreaking" — appeals to emotion not logic
Tripling
"It's unfair, unjust, and un-British" — creates rhythm & emphatic effect
Connectives
Use: Moreover, Furthermore, Crucially — formal persuasive register
Common Examiner Traps avoid these
Don't confuse inference with retrieval. Q1 = what IS said. Q2 = what it SUGGESTS.
Don't analyse techniques without linking to effect. "It's a metaphor" ≠ analysis. "It's a metaphor that conveys..." = analysis
Don't use clichéd comparisons (brave as a lion, cold as ice, worked like a dog). Shows imagination exhaustion.
Don't forget formality match. Casual slang in a formal persuasive letter = tone error; some marks lost.
DO use subjects from the writing prompt in your opening. DO vary sentence lengths. DO proofread.
The Mark-Winning Formula all papers
✓ READING: Find evidence → Name technique → Explain effect → Link to writer's purpose
✓ WRITING: Strong opening hook → Clear organisation (cohesion) → Ambitious vocabulary → Correct SPAG → Punchy ending
✓ LANGUAGE: Show range (don't repeat "good" — use excellent, superb, outstanding, exceptional; don't repeat "said" — use asserted, proclaimed, articulated)
Examiners reward: ambitious language choices, tight analysis, structural clarity, zero errors, adaptation to audience/purpose
