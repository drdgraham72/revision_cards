Haber Process & Equilibrium Q5 · 10 marks
Equation
N₂ + 3H₂ ⇌ 2NH₃ (exothermic)
Conditions
450°C, 200 atm, Fe catalyst — compromise (not max yield)
Fe purpose
Catalyst — speeds both fwd & rev equally; not used up
↓ temperature
Equilibrium shifts RIGHT (→ NH₃); exothermic reaction favoured
↑ pressure
Shifts to fewer moles of gas → RIGHT (4 mol → 2 mol)
Dynamic equil
Fwd rate = rev rate; concentrations constant but both reactions occurring
Colour test
NO₂ (brown) ⇌ N₂O₄ (colourless): ice → paler; hot water → darker

Cathode (−)

Anode (+)

Salts & Crystallisation Q8 · 11 marks
CuO + H₂SO₄
→ CuSO₄ + H₂O (other product = water)
Stop adding CuO
When black powder no longer dissolves (acid used up); filter off excess
Get crystals

Solution → crystal
Particles lose KE · move less · arrangement becomes regular lattice · closer together

Purity, Chromatography & Separation Q9 · 12 marks
Pure substance
Sharp single melting point; range indicates impure
Sample A 160–164
Range = IMPURE; melting point depressed and widened by impurities
Sample B 166
Single value = PURE (but higher than A/C → different substance?)
Sample C 163–165
Range = IMPURE
Rf formula
Rf = dist. spot / dist. solvent front · eg 0.22×2.4 = 0.53 cm
NaCl vs H₂O bp
NaCl = giant ionic lattice, strong electrostatic forces → huge energy to overcome · water = simple molecular, weak IMFs → low bp
Separation method
Distil at 100°C → collect water vapour; NaCl stays in flask (bp 1465°C)
Empirical formula = simplest whole number ratio — count atoms in diagram then divide by HCF
