Every Equation You Need

Rearrange them. Understand them. Own them.

Motion & Forces

Speed
v = s ÷ t
speed = distance ÷ time
Acceleration
a = Δv ÷ t
acceleration = change in velocity ÷ time
Newton's Second Law
F = m a
force = mass × acceleration
Work Done
W = F d
work done = force × distance
Momentum
p = m v
momentum = mass × velocity

Energy

Kinetic Energy
KE = ½mv²
kinetic energy = ½ × mass × velocity²
Gravitational PE
GPE = m g h
grav. potential energy = mass × g × height
Power (from energy)
P = E ÷ t
power = energy transferred ÷ time
Power (from work)
P = W ÷ t
power = work done ÷ time
Efficiency
efficiency = useful output⁄total input × 100
as a percentage — or leave as a decimal (0 to 1)
Specific Heat Capacity
E = mcΔT
energy = mass × SHC × temp change

Waves

Wave Speed
v = f λ
wave speed = frequency × wavelength
Period
T = 1 ÷ f
period = 1 ÷ frequency

Rearranging Algebraically

Whatever you do to one side, do EXACTLY the same to the other
Example: v = fλ — find f
1
Start with the equation v = fλ
2
You want f on its own — it's being multiplied by λ, so divide both sides by λ v ÷ λ = fλ ÷ λ
3
The λ cancels on the right f = v ÷ λ
Example: KE = ½mv² — find v
1
Start with KE = ½mv²
2
Multiply both sides by 2 to clear the ½ 2 × KE = mv²
3
Divide both sides by m 2KE ÷ m = v²
4
Square root both sides v = √(2KE ÷ m)
Example: F = ma — find m
1
Start with F = ma
2
m is multiplied by a, so divide both sides by a F ÷ a = ma ÷ a
3
The a cancels on the right m = F ÷ a

Units Matter

Quantity Unit Symbol Watch Out
Speed metres per second m/s NOT km/h
Acceleration metres per second squared m/s²  
Force newtons N  
Mass kilograms kg NOT grams!
Energy joules J NOT kJ
Power watts W NOT kW
Frequency hertz Hz  
Wavelength metres m NOT cm or nm
Time seconds s NOT minutes
Temp. change degrees Celsius °C  
Unit Conversion Traps
g → kg ÷ 1000
km/h → m/s ÷ 3.6
kJ → J × 1000
kW → W × 1000
minutes → s × 60
hours → s × 3600
cm → m ÷ 100
km → m × 1000
