A-Level Maths — The Mistakes That Cost You Marks Every Year

The wall of shame. Learn these. Never lose these marks again.

1 Calculus Mistakes

×
Forgetting +c on indefinite integrals
Wrong
∫2x dx = x²
Right
∫2x dx = x² + c
Every indefinite integral has infinitely many solutions — the +c accounts for the vertical shift. One mark, every time.
×
Chain rule forgotten — the inner derivative is missing
Wrong
d/dx [sin(3x)] = cos(3x)
Right
d/dx [sin(3x)] = 3cos(3x)
Multiply by the derivative of the inner function. Always. The 3 comes from differentiating 3x.
×
d/dx[cos(x)] — the minus sign that everyone forgets
Wrong
d/dx [cos x] = sin x
Right
d/dx [cos x] = −sin x
Differentiating cos gives MINUS sin. The minus sign is not optional — it changes the entire answer.
×
Integrating sin(kx) — forgetting the 1/k factor
Wrong
∫sin(kx) dx = −cos(kx) + c
Right
∫sin(kx) dx = −(1/k)cos(kx) + c
Integration is the reverse of the chain rule — you must divide by the coefficient of x.
×
Product rule — only differentiating one part
Wrong
d/dx [x²eˣ] = 2xeˣ
Right
d/dx [x²eˣ] = 2xeˣ + x²eˣ
Product rule: d/dx[uv] = u'v + uv'. You need BOTH terms. The wrong answer is just the first term.
×
Integration by parts — choosing wrong u and dv
Remember LIATE for choosing u
Logs → Inverse trig → Algebra → Trig → Exponentials
Pick u from the LEFT of this list. Whatever is leftmost = u.
For ∫x·eˣ dx: x is Algebra, eˣ is Exponential. A comes before E, so u = x.
×
Not changing limits when substituting in definite integrals
Wrong
Sub u = 3x, keep limits as x = 1 to x = 4
Right
Sub u = 3x, new limits: u = 3 to u = 12
When you change variable, the limits change too. Either convert limits to the new variable, or substitute back to x before evaluating.
×
d/dx[eˣ] — treating it like a power rule
Wrong
d/dx [eˣ] = xeˣ−¹
Right
d/dx [eˣ] = eˣ
eˣ differentiates to itself. The power rule xⁿ → nxⁿ−¹ is for x as the BASE, not as the EXPONENT.
×
d/dx[ln x] — getting confused with ln in the result
Wrong
d/dx [ln x] = ln(x)/x
Right
d/dx [ln x] = 1/x
The derivative of ln x is simply 1/x. The ln disappears completely. Don't mix this up with integration.

2 Algebra Mistakes

×
Expanding (a+b)² — the missing middle term
Wrong
(a+b)² = a² + b²
Right
(a+b)² = a² + 2ab + b²
Squaring a bracket means (a+b)(a+b). The 2ab cross term is unavoidable. Always expand in full.
×
Square root does NOT distribute over addition
Wrong
√(a² + b²) = a + b
Right
√(a² + b²) ≠ a + b — leave it as is
Try it: √(9+16) = √25 = 5, but 3+4 = 7. They are NOT the same. There is no simplification.
×
Dividing an inequality by a negative — the sign FLIPS
Wrong
−2x > 6 ⇒ x > −3
Right
−2x > 6 ⇒ x < −3
Dividing or multiplying both sides by a negative number REVERSES the inequality. Always.
×
Completing the square — sign error on the constant
Wrong
x² + 6x = (x+3)² + 9
Right
x² + 6x = (x+3)² − 9
(x+3)² = x² + 6x + 9. To get back to x² + 6x you must SUBTRACT 9. Check by expanding!
×
Forgetting ± in the quadratic formula — there are TWO solutions
Wrong
x = (−b + √(b²−4ac)) / 2a
Right
x = (−b ± √(b²−4ac)) / 2a
A quadratic has up to 2 roots. The ± gives you both. Forgetting one solution = losing marks.
×
Log rules — log(a+b) is NOT log(a) + log(b)
Wrong
log(a + b) = log a + log b
Right
log(ab) = log a + log b
log(a) + log(b) = log(ab), NOT log(a+b). The log of a sum cannot be simplified. Don't confuse addition with multiplication.

3 Trigonometry Mistakes

×
Calculator in wrong mode — degrees vs radians
Wrong
sin(90) = 0.8939... (radians mode!)
Right
sin(90°) = 1 (degrees mode)
Check your calculator mode BEFORE every trig calculation. At A-Level, radians are used more often — but know which one the question wants.
×
sin−¹(x) means arcsin, NOT 1/sin(x)
Wrong
sin−¹(x) = 1/sin(x)
Right
sin−¹(x) = arcsin(x), 1/sin(x) = cosec(x)
The −1 superscript is confusing notation. sin−¹ is the INVERSE function (arcsin). The reciprocal is cosec.
×
Exact values — using calculator in the wrong mode for known angles
Wrong
sin(π/6) typed as sin(30) in radians mode = −0.988...
Right
sin(π/6) = sin(30°) = 1/2
Know your exact values: π/6 = 30°, π/4 = 45°, π/3 = 60°. If the question uses radians, TYPE radians.
×
Solving trig equations — forgetting ALL solutions in the range
Wrong
sin x = 0.5, x = 30° (only one solution!)
Right
sin x = 0.5, x = 30° and x = 150°
Use the CAST diagram to find all solutions in the given range. Trig functions repeat — there is almost always more than one answer.
S (sin+)
A (all+)
T (tan+)
C (cos+)
×
sin²x + cos²x = 1 — what the notation means
Wrong
sin(x²) + cos(x²) = 1
Right
(sin x)² + (cos x)² = 1
sin²x means (sin x)² — square the result. sin(x²) means take the sine of x² — completely different.
×
Radians to degrees conversion — which way round?
Wrong
Radians → degrees: multiply by π/180
Right
Radians → degrees: multiply by 180/π
π rad = 180°. To go from radians to degrees, multiply by 180/π. To go from degrees to radians, multiply by π/180. Remember: π is small (~3.14), degrees are big (180).

4 Statistics Mistakes

×
Normal distribution — using σ² instead of σ in the z-score
Wrong
z = (x − μ) / σ²
Right
z = (x − μ) / σ
The z-score formula uses the standard deviation σ, NOT the variance σ². If the question gives you variance, take the square root first.
×
Continuity correction forgotten when approximating binomial with normal
Wrong
P(X ≤ 10) ≈ P(Z ≤ (10−μ)/σ)
Right
P(X ≤ 10) ≈ P(Z ≤ (10.5−μ)/σ)
Discrete → continuous approximation needs ±0.5 adjustment. ≤ 10 becomes ≤ 10.5, ≥ 10 becomes ≥ 9.5.
×
Hypothesis testing — NEVER write "accept H₀"
Wrong
We accept H₀
Right
Insufficient evidence to reject H₀
You can never prove H₀ is true — you can only fail to find evidence against it. The phrasing matters for marks.
×
Confusing P(X ≤ k) with P(X < k) for discrete distributions
Wrong
P(X < 3) = P(X ≤ 3) for binomial
Right
P(X < 3) = P(X ≤ 2) for discrete
For discrete distributions, P(X < 3) = P(X ≤ 2) because X can only take integer values. The boundary value matters.
×
Binomial: wrong n or p in the calculator
Wrong
P("at least 3 failures") using p = P(success)
Right
Redefine: p = P(failure), or use 1−P(X≤2)
Before using binomial tables or calculator: identify what counts as "success", what n is, and what p is. Double-check before pressing =.

5 Mechanics Mistakes

×
Missing g — weight is NOT the same as mass
Wrong
Weight of 5 kg object = 5 N
Right
Weight = mg = 5 × 9.8 = 49 N
Mass is in kg, weight is a force in N. W = mg. Always multiply by g (9.8 m/s²).
×
Sign convention — not being consistent with positive direction
Wrong
Mixing up: some forces positive up, some positive down
Right
State direction: "Taking up as positive" — then stick to it
Pick a positive direction, write it down, and use it CONSISTENTLY. If up is positive, g = −9.8. Mixing signs = wrong answer.
×
SUVAT when acceleration is NOT constant
Wrong
a = f(t), using v = u + at
Right
a = f(t), use v = ∫a dt (calculus!)
SUVAT equations ONLY work when acceleration is constant. If a varies with time, you must integrate or differentiate.
×
Resolving forces — sin vs cos on inclined planes
Wrong
Component down the slope = mg cosθ
Right
Component down the slope = mg sinθ
Draw the triangle every time. The component parallel to the slope is mg sinθ. The component perpendicular (normal) is mg cosθ. Always sketch it.
×
Projectiles — v = 0 at max height applies to VERTICAL component only
Wrong
At max height: total velocity = 0
Right
At max height: vₕ = 0, but vₓ is unchanged
At maximum height the vertical velocity is zero, but the horizontal velocity stays constant (no air resistance). The object is still moving!

6 General Exam Technique

×
"Show that" — you MUST show every step
Wrong
Show that x = 5. Answer: "x = 5 ✔"
Right
Show every algebraic step leading to x = 5
In "show that" questions, the answer is given. The marks are for the WORKING. Skip a step = lose a mark.
×
"Give exact answer" — no decimals allowed
Wrong
Area = 3.14159...
Right
Area = π (or 2√3, or ln 5, etc.)
"Exact" means leave in terms of π, surds (√), ln, fractions. The moment you reach for the calculator, you've gone wrong.
×
"Hence" vs "Hence or otherwise" — they mean different things
Wrong
"Hence find..." → starting from scratch
Right
"Hence" = you MUST use your previous answer
"Hence" = use the result you just found. "Hence or otherwise" = use it if you can, or find another method. Ignoring "hence" wastes time and may lose marks.
×
Not showing working — even correct answers lose method marks
Wrong
Answer: x = 7 (no working shown)
Right
3x + 2 = 23, 3x = 21, x = 7
A 4-mark question with just a final answer: 1 mark at best. Show your method = safety net even if the answer is wrong.
×
Premature rounding — keep full precision until the final step
Wrong
Midway: z = 1.33, then use 1.33 in next step
Right
Keep z = 1.33333... in calculator, round at the END
Rounding too early accumulates errors. Use your calculator's ANS button to carry full precision through. Only round the FINAL answer to the requested d.p./s.f.
Every mistake above is free marks thrown away.
Learn them. Never make them again.
These are not hard maths mistakes — they are careless mistakes. And careless mistakes are the ones you CAN fix.
