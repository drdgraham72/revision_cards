Simple Harmonic Motion

A-Level Physics • The complete picture, one card

The Definition

Acceleration is proportional to displacement AND directed towards the equilibrium position
a = −ω²x
The minus sign means the acceleration always points back towards equilibrium.
When x is positive, a is negative. When x is negative, a is positive. Always restoring.
ω = 2πf = 2π/T
ω is the angular frequency (rad/s). f is frequency (Hz). T is period (s).

The Three Equations

Displacement
x = A cos(ωt)
Starts at maximum if released from rest (t=0, x=A)
Velocity
v = −Aω sin(ωt)
Zero at the extremes, maximum at the centre
Acceleration
a = −Aω² cos(ωt)
Maximum at the extremes, zero at the centre
Max Speed
vmax = Aω
Max Acceleration
amax = Aω²

The Graphs — Phase Relationships

x, v, and a vs time — stacked to show phase
When x is at max, v = 0 and a is at max negative. • Velocity leads displacement by 90°. Acceleration is 180° ahead (anti-phase with x).

The Velocity Equation They Always Forget

v = ±ω√(A² − x²)
At equilibrium (x = 0)
v = ±ωA
Maximum speed — all energy is kinetic
At extreme (x = A)
v = 0
Momentarily stationary — all energy is potential
Worked Example: A pendulum has A = 0.05 m and T = 2.0 s. Find v when x = 0.03 m.
1
Find ω ω = 2π/T = 2π/2.0 = π = 3.14 rad/s
2
Use v = ω√(A² − x²) v = 3.14 × √(0.05² − 0.03²)
3
Evaluate v = 3.14 × √(0.0025 − 0.0009) = 3.14 × 0.04 = 0.126 m/s
v = 0.13 m/s (2 s.f.)

Energy in SHM

Kinetic Energy
KE = ½mω²(A²−x²)
Max at centre, zero at extremes
Potential Energy
PE = ½mω²x²
Zero at centre, max at extremes
Total Energy
E = ½mω²A²
CONSTANT — always the same
At Equilibrium (x = 0)
ALL kinetic energy
Zero potential energy
At Extremes (x = ±A)
ALL potential energy
Zero kinetic energy
Energy vs Displacement
KE + PE = constant at every point. The two parabolas are mirror images. Where one is max, the other is zero.

Damping & Resonance

Light Damping
Amplitude decreases gradually over many oscillations. Frequency stays (approximately) the same. Exponential decay envelope.
Heavy Damping
Returns to equilibrium slowly without oscillating. Takes a long time. Think: mass in treacle.
Critical Damping
Returns to equilibrium as quickly as possible without oscillating. The sweet spot. Used in car suspension and door closers.
Overdamping
Returns to equilibrium without oscillating but slower than critical damping. More resistive force than needed.
Resonance
fdriving = fnatural → maximum amplitude
When the driving frequency matches the natural frequency, energy transfer is most efficient and amplitude reaches a maximum.
More damping = lower and broader resonance peak. Less damping = taller and sharper peak.

SHM vs SUVAT — The Critical Distinction

Do Not Confuse These
SUVAT
Constant acceleration.
a does not change.
v = u + at, s = ut + ½at²
Projectiles, free fall, linear motion.
SHM
Acceleration changes continuously.
a = −ω²x (depends on position).
x = A cos(ωt), v = −Aω sin(ωt)
Pendulums, springs, oscillations.
NEVER use SUVAT for SHM!
a = −ω²x
x = Acos • v = −Aωsin • a = −Aω²cos
v = ±ω√(A² − x²)
The definition, the three equations, and the velocity formula. That's SHM.
