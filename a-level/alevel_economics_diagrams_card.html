Diagram 01

Supply & Demand

Use for: market equilibrium, price mechanism, consumer/producer surplus

Diagram 02

Negative Externality (Production)

Use for: market failure, pollution, government intervention, taxation
Key labels
MPC = Marginal Private Cost MSC = Marginal Social Cost MPB = MSB Overproduction at Qfm
Diagram 03

Positive Externality (Consumption)

Use for: merit goods, education, healthcare, subsidies
Key labels
MSC = MPC MPB = Marginal Private Benefit MSB = Marginal Social Benefit Underproduction at Qfm
Diagram 04

Indirect Tax

Use for: government intervention, demerit goods, tax incidence, cigarettes/alcohol
Key labels
S+tax shifts up Tax revenue = rectangle Consumer pays P1→P2 Producer receives Pp
Diagram 05

Subsidy

Use for: merit goods, government intervention, reducing prices, increasing output
Key labels
S−sub shifts right/down Consumer pays P2 (lower) Producer receives Ps (higher) Green area = cost to government
Diagram 06

Monopoly

Use for: market structure, profit maximisation, efficiency, regulation
Key labels
AR = Demand MR below AR MC through AC min Profit = (Pm−AC) × Qm
Diagram 07

Perfect Competition (Short Run & Long Run)

Use for: market structure comparison, efficiency, normal vs supernormal profit
Key labels
AR = MR = D = P (horizontal, price taker) MC crosses AR at profit-max Q SR: P > AC = supernormal profit LR: P = AC(min) = normal profit only
Diagram 08

Labour Market

Use for: wage determination, minimum wage, monopsony, trade unions
Key labels
DL = MRP (Marginal Revenue Product) SL = Labour Supply We, Qe = Equilibrium wage & employment

Macroeconomics

6 diagrams
Diagram 09

AD/AS (Keynesian)

Use for: spare capacity, recession, output gap, fiscal policy
Key labels
LRAS = horizontal then vertical AD = Aggregate Demand Spare capacity = no inflation zone Yf = Full employment output
Diagram 10

AD/AS (Classical)

Use for: long-run equilibrium, supply-side policy, monetarist view
Key labels
LRAS = vertical at Yf SRAS = upward sloping AD = Aggregate Demand PL = Equilibrium price level
Diagram 11

AD Shift Right (Demand-Pull Inflation)

Use for: economic growth, inflation, consumer confidence, fiscal stimulus
Key labels
AD1 → AD2 (shift right) PL rises = demand-pull inflation Y rises = economic growth
Diagram 12

LRAS Shift Right (Economic Growth)

Use for: supply-side policies, education, infrastructure, productivity gains
Key labels
LRAS1 → LRAS2 (shift right) AD unchanged Output rises Y1→Y2, price falls Non-inflationary growth
Diagram 13

Multiplier Effect

Use for: fiscal policy, government spending, injections, MPC/MPS
Key labels
ΔG = initial injection k × ΔG = multiplied AD shift ΔY > ΔG (output rises by more)
Diagram 14

Phillips Curve

Use for: inflation vs unemployment trade-off, monetary/fiscal policy dilemma
Key labels
SRPC = Short-Run Phillips Curve A = low unemp, high inflation B = high unemp, low inflation Inverse relationship (short run)

Tariff Diagram

Use for: protectionism, free trade, WTO, domestic industry, deadweight loss
Key labels
Pw = World price Pw+t = Price with tariff DWL = Deadweight loss triangles Gov't revenue = rectangle Q2 to Q3 Domestic production rises Q1→Q2, consumption falls Q4→Q3
Diagram 16

Kinked Demand Curve

Use for: oligopoly, price rigidity, interdependence, non-price competition
Key labels
Kinked D at current price P* MR has discontinuous gap MC can shift within gap — price stays at P*
Diagram 17

J-Curve

Use for: depreciation, current account, Marshall-Lerner condition, exchange rates
Key labels
Initial worsening (price effect) Later improvement (volume effect) Shaped like letter J
Diagram 18

Lorenz Curve & Gini Coefficient

Use for: income inequality, wealth distribution, redistribution policy
Key labels
Line of equality (perfect 45°) Lorenz curve bows below A = area between curves Gini = A/(A+B) — closer to 1 = more unequal
