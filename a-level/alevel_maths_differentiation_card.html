Answer Yes or No. Three questions maximum.
The Chain Rule
\(\dfrac{dy}{dx} = \dfrac{dy}{du} \times \dfrac{du}{dx}\)
"Differentiate the outside, keep the inside, multiply by the derivative of the inside"
1 Identify the inner function u and the outer function y
2 Differentiate the outer function (keeping inner as u)
3 Differentiate the inner function
4 Multiply them together

Example 1: \(\sin(3x)\)

\(u = 3x, \quad y = \sin(u)\)
\(\dfrac{dy}{du} = \cos(u), \quad \dfrac{du}{dx} = 3\)
\(\dfrac{dy}{dx} = \cos(3x) \times 3 = 3\cos(3x)\) ✓

Example 2: \((2x + 1)^5\)

\(u = 2x + 1, \quad y = u^5\)
\(\dfrac{dy}{du} = 5u^4, \quad \dfrac{du}{dx} = 2\)
\(\dfrac{dy}{dx} = 5(2x+1)^4 \times 2 = 10(2x+1)^4\) ✓

Example 3: \(e^{x^2}\)

\(u = x^2, \quad y = e^u\)
\(\dfrac{dy}{du} = e^u, \quad \dfrac{du}{dx} = 2x\)
\(\dfrac{dy}{dx} = e^{x^2} \times 2x = 2x \cdot e^{x^2}\) ✓
The Product Rule
\(\dfrac{d(uv)}{dx} = u\dfrac{dv}{dx} + v\dfrac{du}{dx}\)
"first × d(second) + second × d(first)"
1 Label u and v (the two parts being multiplied)
2 Find \(\dfrac{du}{dx}\) and \(\dfrac{dv}{dx}\) separately
3 Apply the formula: \(u \cdot \dfrac{dv}{dx} + v \cdot \dfrac{du}{dx}\)

Example 1: \(x^2 \cdot \sin(x)\)

\(u = x^2, \quad v = \sin(x)\)
\(\dfrac{du}{dx} = 2x, \quad \dfrac{dv}{dx} = \cos(x)\)
\(\dfrac{dy}{dx} = x^2 \cdot \cos(x) + \sin(x) \cdot 2x\)
\(= x^2\cos(x) + 2x\sin(x)\) ✓

Example 2: \(e^x \cdot \ln(x)\)

\(u = e^x, \quad v = \ln(x)\)
\(\dfrac{du}{dx} = e^x, \quad \dfrac{dv}{dx} = \frac{1}{x}\)
\(\dfrac{dy}{dx} = e^x \cdot \frac{1}{x} + \ln(x) \cdot e^x\)
\(= e^x\!\left(\frac{1}{x} + \ln(x)\right)\) ✓
The Quotient Rule
\(\dfrac{d(u/v)}{dx} = \dfrac{v\dfrac{du}{dx} - u\dfrac{dv}{dx}}{v^2}\)
"lo d-hi minus hi d-lo, over lo-lo"
Or rewrite \(u/v\) as \(u \cdot v^{-1}\) and use product + chain rule instead.
1 Label u (top) and v (bottom)
2 Find \(\dfrac{du}{dx}\) and \(\dfrac{dv}{dx}\) separately
3 Apply: \(\dfrac{v \cdot \frac{du}{dx} - u \cdot \frac{dv}{dx}}{v^2}\)

Example 1: \(\dfrac{\sin(x)}{x^2}\)

\(u = \sin(x), \quad v = x^2\)
\(\dfrac{du}{dx} = \cos(x), \quad \dfrac{dv}{dx} = 2x\)
\(\dfrac{dy}{dx} = \dfrac{x^2 \cdot \cos(x) - \sin(x) \cdot 2x}{x^4}\)
\(= \dfrac{x\cos(x) - 2\sin(x)}{x^3}\) ✓

Example 2: \(\dfrac{x+1}{x-1}\)

\(u = x+1, \quad v = x-1\)
\(\dfrac{du}{dx} = 1, \quad \dfrac{dv}{dx} = 1\)
\(\dfrac{dy}{dx} = \dfrac{(x-1) \cdot 1 - (x+1) \cdot 1}{(x-1)^2}\)
\(= \dfrac{x-1-x-1}{(x-1)^2} = \dfrac{-2}{(x-1)^2}\) ✓

Standard Results — Must Know

\(f(x)\) \(f'(x)\)
\(x^n\) \(nx^{n-1}\)
\(e^x\) \(e^x\)
\(e^{kx}\) \(ke^{kx}\)
\(\ln(x)\) \(\dfrac{1}{x}\)
\(\sin(x)\) \(\cos(x)\)
\(\cos(x)\) \(-\sin(x)\)
\(\tan(x)\) \(\sec^2(x)\)
\(\sin(kx)\) \(k\cos(kx)\)
\(\cos(kx)\) \(-k\sin(kx)\)

Common Mistakes

  • Forgetting the chain rule on composite functions:
    \(\frac{d}{dx}[\sin(3x)]\) = \(\cos(3x)\) → \(3\cos(3x)\)
  • Sign errors with \(\cos \rightarrow -\sin\):
    \(\frac{d}{dx}[\cos(x)]\) = \(\sin(x)\) → \(-\sin(x)\)
  • Mixing up product and quotient rule formulas: product has a +, quotient has a − (and the square on the bottom).
  • Not simplifying the answer: always factorise and cancel where possible — marks are often awarded for the simplified form.
