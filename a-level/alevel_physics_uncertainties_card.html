Uncertainties — The Rules

A-Level Physics • Every rule, every trap, one card

The Three Types

Absolute Uncertainty
± 0.5 mm
Same units as the measurement.
It's the raw “plus or minus”.
Fractional Uncertainty
0.5 / 50.0 = 0.01
No units. Just a ratio.
Uncertainty ÷ measured value.
Percentage Uncertainty
0.01 × 100 = 1%
Fractional × 100.
The one you use most often.

Combining Uncertainties

Adding or Subtracting Quantities
ADD the absolute uncertainties
Δ(A±B) = ΔA + ΔB
Example: Length = end − start
end = 84.0 ± 0.5 mm, start = 10.0 ± 0.5 mm Length = 74.0 ± 1.0 mm Both absolute uncertainties add: 0.5 + 0.5 = 1.0 mm
Multiplying or Dividing Quantities
ADD the percentage uncertainties
%Δ(A×B) = %ΔA + %ΔB
Example: Speed = distance / time
d = 100 m ± 2%, t = 10 s ± 3% %Δspeed = 2% + 3% = 5% speed = 10 m/s ± 5% = 10 ± 0.5 m/s
Raising to a Power
MULTIPLY the percentage uncertainty by the power
%Δ(An) = n × %ΔA
Example: Volume of a sphere: V = 4/3 πr³
If %Δr = 2%, what is %ΔV? %ΔV = 3 × 2% = 6% The power of 3 on r means you triple the percentage uncertainty.

Reading Instruments

Ruler
± 0.5 mm
Half the smallest division (1 mm). Apply at each end if measuring a length — total ±1 mm.
Stopwatch (Human)
± 0.2–0.5 s
Reaction time dominates, not the instrument precision (0.01 s). Use ±0.2 s minimum per press.
Digital Instrument
± 1 last digit
A balance reading 24.56 g has uncertainty ±0.01 g. A voltmeter reading 3.2 V has ±0.1 V.
Multiple Readings
range / 2
Take the spread: uncertainty = (max − min) / 2. This captures the random variation in your data.

Graphs and Uncertainties

Error Bars
Plot the absolute uncertainty as vertical (and/or horizontal) bars on each data point. The bar extends ±Δy above and below the point.
Best Fit & Worst Acceptable Lines
The best fit line goes through the data as closely as possible.
The worst acceptable lines are the steepest and shallowest lines that still pass through all the error bars.
Gradient Uncertainty
Δgradient = (max gradient − min gradient) / 2 Use the worst acceptable lines to find the steepest and shallowest gradients.
Y-Intercept Uncertainty
Read the y-intercept from each worst acceptable line. Δintercept = (max intercept − min intercept) / 2

The Mistakes That Lose Marks

×
Confusing absolute and percentage uncertainties — you can't add percentage uncertainties when you're adding/subtracting quantities!
×
Forgetting to multiply the percentage uncertainty by the power — if r has 2% uncertainty, r³ has 6%, not 2%.
×
Not stating the uncertainty with the correct units — absolute uncertainties MUST have units, percentage uncertainties MUST NOT.
×
Too many significant figures in the uncertainty — uncertainties should be 1 or 2 s.f. maximum. If your uncertainty is ±0.4732 mm, round it to ±0.5 mm.
Add/Sub → add absolutes
Mult/Div → add percentages
Powers → multiply percentage by n
Three rules. That's the whole game. Learn which one to use, and you'll never drop marks on uncertainties again.
