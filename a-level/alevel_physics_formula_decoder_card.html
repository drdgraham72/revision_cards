3. SUVAT Decision Tree — Which of the 5 equations?

Identify your 3 knowns and 1 unknown. Find the equation that contains all 4.
Each SUVAT equation uses 4 of the 5 variables (s, u, v, a, t).
The trick: find which variable you don’t need — then use the equation that’s missing it.
Missing s
v = u + at
has: u, v, a, t
Missing v
s = ut + ½at²
has: s, u, a, t
Missing u
s = vt - ½at²
has: s, v, a, t
Missing a
s = ½(u+v)t
has: s, u, v, t
Missing t
v² = u² + 2as
has: s, u, v, a

4. Unit Checks — The Quick Sanity Test

The Reality Check
If your answer should be in metres and you got kg — something’s wrong.
If you got a negative speed — something’s wrong.
If an electron has the mass of a planet — something’s very wrong.
Always check: does my answer make physical sense?
Common Unit Conversions
mm → m ÷ 1000
cm → m ÷ 100
km → m × 1000
g → kg ÷ 1000
mA → A ÷ 1000
kW → W × 1000
MHz → Hz × 10&sup6;
nm → m × 10⁻&sup9;
μm → m × 10⁻&sup6;
Standard Form — Get Comfortable
A-Level physics lives in standard form. Know your powers of 10 cold.
Speed of light: 3.00 × 10&sup8; m/s • Electron charge: 1.60 × 10⁻¹&sup9; C • Planck’s constant: 6.63 × 10⁻³&sup4; J·s
Practise entering these on your calculator until it’s automatic.

5. The Golden Rule

The One Rule That Prevents Most Mistakes
Write the equation.
Substitute the numbers.
THEN rearrange if needed.
Never rearrange first — you’ll make sign errors.
Read the question. Spot the keywords. Pick the equation.
Write it. Substitute. Rearrange. Calculate. Units. Done.
