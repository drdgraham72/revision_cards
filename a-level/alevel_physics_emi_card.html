Electromagnetic Induction

A-Level Physics • Faraday's & Lenz's Laws

The Big Idea

THE WHOLE TOPIC IN THREE LINES
A changing magnetic flux through a circuit induces an EMF
The induced EMF opposes the change causing it
That's it. Everything else is details.

Magnetic Flux

STEP BY STEP
Φ = BA cosθ
Φ
Magnetic flux — units: Weber (Wb)
B
Magnetic flux density — units: Tesla (T)
A
Area of coil — units: m²
θ
Angle between B and the NORMAL to the coil surface
The angle trap
  • When B is perpendicular to the coil face: θ = 0°, cosθ = 1, so Φ = BA (maximum flux)
  • When B is parallel to the coil face: θ = 90°, cosθ = 0, so Φ = 0 (no flux)
  • θ is measured from the normal (the line sticking out perpendicular to the coil face), NOT from the surface itself

Flux Linkage

N TURNS
Flux linkage = NΦ

N = number of turns on the coil. Φ = flux through each turn.

Units: Wb turns (or just Wb, depending on the exam board).

A coil with 200 turns in a flux of 0.05 Wb has flux linkage = 200 × 0.05 = 10 Wb turns.

Faraday's Law

THE EQUATION
ε = −N dΦ/dt

The induced EMF equals the negative rate of change of flux linkage.

How to get a bigger EMF
  • More turns (N) on the coil
  • Stronger magnetic field (bigger B, so bigger Φ)
  • Faster change (bigger dΦ/dt — move the magnet faster, spin the coil faster)
  • Larger coil area (bigger A, so bigger Φ)

Lenz's Law

THE DIRECTION

The induced current flows in a direction to OPPOSE the change causing it.

Magnet pushed INTO coil
Flux through coil increases ↓ Induced current creates a magnetic field to REPEL the magnet
Magnet pulled OUT of coil
Flux through coil decreases ↓ Induced current creates a magnetic field to ATTRACT the magnet
Why does it oppose?
Conservation of energy. If the induced current HELPED the change, you'd get energy for free. You have to do WORK to push the magnet in — that work is what generates the electrical energy.

Generators & Transformers

APPLICATIONS

AC Generator

Coil rotating in a magnetic field
ε = BANω sin(ωt)
EMF is sinusoidal — alternates positive and negative
Peak EMF = BANω (when coil is parallel to field, flux changing fastest)
EMF = 0 when coil is perpendicular to field (flux momentarily not changing)
ω = 2πf = angular frequency

Transformers

Changing flux links two coils via an iron core
Vs/Vp = Ns/Np = Ip/Is
Assumes 100% efficiency (ideal transformer)
Real transformers: energy lost as heat in coils and core
Efficiency = (VsIs)/(VpIp) × 100%

Step-Up

Ns > Np
Increases voltage
Decreases current

Step-Down

Ns < Np
Decreases voltage
Increases current

The Mistakes

AVOID THESE
Confusing Φ (flux) with NΦ (flux linkage)
Φ is the flux through one loop. NΦ is the total flux linkage for a coil of N turns. Faraday's law uses NΦ.
Getting θ wrong in Φ = BA cosθ
θ is the angle to the NORMAL (perpendicular to the coil face), NOT the angle to the surface. When field goes straight through the coil, θ = 0.
Forgetting the minus sign
The minus sign in ε = −NdΦ/dt represents Lenz's law. It tells you the induced EMF opposes the change. Drop it and you lose the physics.
Using Vs/Vp = Ns/Np when not 100% efficient
The turns ratio always gives the voltage ratio. But Ip/Is = Ns/Np only holds for an ideal transformer. For real ones, use efficiency = Pout/Pin.
