Waves: The Complete Picture

GCSE Physics Paper 1 — frequency, wavelength, amplitude & the EM spectrum

Transverse Waves

Oscillation PERPENDICULAR to direction of travel
Oscillation at right angles to the wave direction
Has crests (peaks) and troughs
Can be polarised
Light, water waves, EM waves, waves on a string

Longitudinal Waves

Oscillation PARALLEL to direction of travel
Oscillation along the same direction as the wave
Has compressions and rarefactions
Cannot be polarised
Sound waves, ultrasound, P-waves (earthquakes)

The Three Things Students Confuse

Get these right and the rest follows
A

Amplitude

Height from rest position to peak. Bigger amplitude = louder sound or brighter light.
Measured in metres (m)
λ

Wavelength

Distance from one peak to the next (or one compression to the next). One full cycle.
Measured in metres (m)
f

Frequency

How many waves pass a point per second. More waves = higher pitch/frequency.
Measured in Hertz (Hz)

The Wave Equation

v = f × λ wave speed (m/s) = frequency (Hz) × wavelength (m)
T = 1 / f period (s) = 1 / frequency (Hz)
Worked Examples
A wave has frequency 50 Hz and wavelength 4 m. Calculate the wave speed.
v = f × λ = 50 × 4 = 200 m/s
Sound travels at 340 m/s. Its frequency is 680 Hz. Calculate the wavelength.
λ = v / f = 340 / 680 = 0.5 m
A wave has a period of 0.02 s. What is its frequency?
f = 1 / T = 1 / 0.02 = 50 Hz

Reflection

Angle of incidence = Angle of reflection
Both angles are measured from the normal (the line perpendicular to the surface), NOT from the surface itself.

Refraction

Wave changes speed → changes direction
Less dense → more dense: bends TOWARDS normal (slows down).
More dense → less dense: bends AWAY from normal (speeds up).

Common Exam Mistakes

✗ Mixing up amplitude and wavelength
✓ Amplitude = height from rest. Wavelength = distance between peaks.
✗ "Waves carry matter from place to place"
✓ Waves transfer ENERGY, not matter. The medium oscillates but doesn't travel.
✗ Forgetting all EM waves travel at the same speed
✓ ALL electromagnetic waves travel at 3 × 10&sup8; m/s in a vacuum.
✗ Measuring the angle from the surface
✓ Always measure from the NORMAL (perpendicular to the surface).
✗ Confusing frequency (Hz) with period (s)
✓ They are inverses: f = 1/T and T = 1/f. Frequency = waves per second. Period = seconds per wave.
✗ Thinking higher frequency means longer wavelength
✓ Higher frequency = SHORTER wavelength (if speed is constant). They are inversely proportional.
