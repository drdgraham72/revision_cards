Trigonometry: Which Method Do I Use?

SOHCAHTOA • Sine Rule • Cosine Rule • Everything you need

Step 1: Which Method?

Ask yourself these questions IN ORDER
Is it a RIGHT-ANGLED triangle?
YES
Use SOHCAHTOA sin, cos, or tan
NO
Do you know a SIDE and its OPPOSITE ANGLE?
YES
Sine Rule a/sinA = b/sinB
NO
Cosine Rule a² = b² + c² − 2bc cosA

SOHCAHTOA — The Big Three

Only for RIGHT-ANGLED triangles
SOH
sin(θ) = O / H
Sin = Opposite ÷ Hypotenuse
CAH
cos(θ) = A / H
Cos = Adjacent ÷ Hypotenuse
TOA
tan(θ) = O / A
Tan = Opposite ÷ Adjacent

Finding a Side

1. Label the triangle (O, A, H)
2. Pick the ratio that uses the side you want and the side you know
3. Substitute and rearrange to solve

e.g. if you know H and want O:
sin(θ) = O/H → O = H × sin(θ)

Finding an Angle

1. Label the triangle (O, A, H)
2. Pick the ratio that uses the two sides you know
3. Calculate the fraction, then use INVERSE:

θ = sin¹(O/H)
θ = cos¹(A/H)
θ = tan¹(O/A)
(That's the SHIFT button on your calculator)

The Right Triangle

Different angle? O and A swap!

Labelling — The Step Everyone Skips

Hypotenuse (H)

Always opposite the right angle.
Always the longest side.
Never changes — it's always the same side regardless of which angle you're using.

Opposite (O) & Adjacent (A)

These depend on which angle you're working with:

Opposite: the side directly across from your angle
Adjacent: the side next to your angle (that isn't the hypotenuse)

Change the angle → O and A swap!

SOHCAHTOA Worked Examples

Example 1: Find the length of side x (finding a side)
1
Right-angled triangle. Angle = 35°, Hypotenuse = 12 cm. Find the Opposite side.
2
I have H, I want O → that's SOH sin(35°) = x / 12
3
Rearrange: x = 12 × sin(35°) x = 12 × 0.5736 = 6.88 cm
x = 6.88 cm
Example 2: Find angle θ (finding an angle)
1
Right-angled triangle. Opposite = 7 cm, Adjacent = 10 cm. Find the angle.
2
I have O and A → that's TOA tan(θ) = 7 / 10 = 0.7
3
Use INVERSE tan (shift + tan on calculator): θ = tan¹(0.7) = 35.0°
θ = 35.0°
Sine Rule
USE WHEN: Non-right-angled triangle + you know a side AND its opposite angle (an angle-side pair)
a / sin A = b / sin B = c / sin C
To find a side: a = (b × sin A) / sin B   |   To find an angle: sin A = (a × sin B) / b
Sine Rule Example: Find side b
1
Triangle (not right-angled): A = 40°, a = 8 cm, B = 75°. Find b.
2
I know a side-angle pair (a and A) + another angle → Sine Rule a / sin A = b / sin B
3
Substitute: 8 / sin 40° = b / sin 75° b = (8 × sin 75°) / sin 40°
4
Calculate: b = (8 × 0.9659) / 0.6428 = 12.02 cm
b = 12.0 cm
Cosine Rule
USE WHEN: Non-right-angled triangle + you know TWO SIDES and the INCLUDED ANGLE, or ALL THREE SIDES (to find an angle)
a² = b² + c² − 2bc cos A
To find an angle: cos A = (b² + c² − a²) / 2bc   then   A = cos¹(...)
Cosine Rule Example: Find side a
1
Triangle: b = 6 cm, c = 9 cm, included angle A = 52°. Find a.
2
Two sides + included angle, no side-angle pair → Cosine Rule a² = b² + c² − 2bc cos A
3
Substitute: a² = 6² + 9² − 2(6)(9) cos 52° a² = 36 + 81 − 108 × 0.6157
4
Calculate: a² = 117 − 66.50 = 50.50 a = √50.50 = 7.11 cm
a = 7.11 cm
Area of a Triangle (with Sine)
USE WHEN: You know TWO SIDES and the INCLUDED ANGLE — works on any triangle
Area = ½ ab sin C
a and b are the two sides • C is the angle BETWEEN them

Common Mistakes

Wrong Calculator Mode
Your calculator must be in DEGREES mode, not radians! Look for a tiny "D" on screen. If you see "R" or "RAD" — change it before you start.
Mislabelling O / A / H
Opposite and Adjacent depend on which angle you're using. Always label relative to your angle, not just by position. The hypotenuse never changes.
SOHCAHTOA on Non-Right Triangles
SOHCAHTOA only works on right-angled triangles. No right angle? You need the Sine Rule or Cosine Rule instead.
Forgetting Inverse Trig
If you get sin(θ) = 0.5 and write "θ = 0.5" — NO! You need sin¹(0.5) = 30°. Use SHIFT + sin/cos/tan on your calculator.
Right angle? → SOHCAHTOA
Got a pair? → Sine Rule
No pair? → Cosine Rule
Label the triangle first. Check your calculator is in degrees. Show your working. You've got this.
